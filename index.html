The Problem

The tools are here. The leadership isn't.

Your team is already using AI. Engineers prompt models. Someone built a prototype over a weekend. There's a pilot somewhere. Maybe two.

But nobody's answered the harder questions. Which processes should you automate and which should you rethink entirely? Where does AI create genuine competitive advantage — and where is it just faster busywork? What should you stop investing in? And who in your organisation actually owns this?

Who in your organisation would know if the AI output was wrong?

If nobody can answer that clearly, you don't have an AI strategy. You have a collection of experiments.

This is happening everywhere, not just in software.

On go-to-market, AI is eating search, automating sales end to end, and collapsing finance workflows into agents. Danfoss used agentic AI to automate 80% of its transaction decisions, cutting customer response time from 42 hours to near-instant.

On product, agents and LLMs are collapsing SaaS tool stacks. Your users won't click through five screens when an agent handles it in a sentence. The question isn't whether your product is affected — it's how much of it still needs to exist.

On production and operations, the human inspector, the manual scheduler, the break-fix maintenance crew — all being replaced. AI vision systems achieve defect escape rates below 0.1%, compared to 5–15% for manual inspection. Over 40% of manufacturers are upgrading to AI-driven scheduling. Digital twins cut commissioning errors by 67%. Predictive maintenance reduces unplanned downtime by 43%.

These questions don't live in engineering. They live in leadership. And most leadership teams don't have anyone who can hold the technology, the strategy, and the organisational change in one conversation.

What Changes When I Show Up

This isn't a list of deliverables. It's what actually happens.

Your roadmap gets honest.

30–50% of what you're building shouldn't exist. We find it, cut it, and redirect the energy toward what matters. You stop debating low-value features and start validating the right bets.

You ship in days, not sprints.

When AI handles the scaffolding, your team focuses on the hard problems. Experiments run faster. Bad ideas die quicker. The cycle from "what if" to "here's the data" collapses.

Product and engineering act as one team.

No more handoffs. No more "engineering doesn't understand the product" or "product doesn't understand the constraints." The people building understand the why. And that changes everything downstream.

Your leaders level up.

The CTO stops firefighting. The founder steps out of the weeds. Key people grow into roles that didn't exist six months ago. I build capability that stays after I leave.

AI gets governed, not just deployed.

Clear rules on what AI touches, what it doesn't, and who's accountable when the output is wrong. Including where you stand under the EU AI Act — not because a regulator is coming tomorrow, but because your clients and partners will ask.

Less theatre, more decisions.

Fewer pilots that go nowhere. Fewer "AI strategy" decks that sit in a drawer. More hard choices made, documented, and executed. If it doesn't change how you work, it wasn't strategy.

How I Work

Every engagement builds your capability, not your dependency on me.

Diagnostic

"Where do we actually stand?"

AI Readiness Diagnostic — 2–4 weeks

Your team has opinions. Your board has questions. Nobody has the same picture.

  • Where AI creates real advantage in your specific business — not generic use cases
  • What your team has already tried, what worked, what stalled and why
  • Whether your organisation is actually ready — skills, data, processes, governance
  • Honest assessment of risk: what happens if you do nothing vs. what happens if you move wrong

What you get

A clear, shared picture. No jargon. Here's where you stand, here's what's at stake, here's where to focus first.

Strategy

"What do we actually do?"

Strategy & Hard Choices — 2–4 weeks

You know the landscape is shifting. Now you need to decide — together.

Starts with a facilitated AI Strategy Workshop — a card deck-based session where your leadership team builds a shared vocabulary, maps opportunities to your actual business, and confronts forced choices: build vs. buy, automate vs. augment, invest vs. let go. The workshop surfaces the real disagreements and assumptions that would otherwise stay hidden until they become expensive.

Then we translate those decisions into a prioritised plan: what to build, what to stop, and in what order. Not a 12-month roadmap — those are fiction. A 90-day action plan with clear ownership.

What you get

Alignment that sticks. A plan your team actually believes in because they built it — with real decisions behind it, not wishful thinking.

The AI Strategy Workshop can also run as a standalone engagement — a natural entry point and a way to see how I work.

Embedded

"Help us actually change."

Embedded Transformation — 3–6 months

You have the strategy. Now the work starts.

  • Embedded with the team 1–2 days/week
  • Coaching product and engineering leads through new ways of building
  • Redesigning workflows, roles, and decision rights around AI
  • Regular checkpoints with leadership — course-correcting, not just reporting

What you get

Actual change. Not a plan on a shelf. A team that builds differently, decides faster, and doesn't need me to keep going.

Principles

How I lead.

Trust first, then challenge.

Give people ambitious problems and the space to solve them. I've seen interns build production products and junior engineers become innovation leaders — when you trust them enough to try.

Outcomes over technical elegance.

Client value comes first. The most elegant architecture means nothing if it doesn't solve a real problem.

Build for now, not for a future that won't arrive.

In the age of AI, don't over-engineer for a three-year horizon. Build what's needed, ship it, learn, adapt. The companies that win aren't the ones with the best plan — they're the ones that adapt fastest.

Lead through synthesis, not authority.

I build alignment by drawing out and integrating diverse perspectives. Better decisions, deeper buy-in.
