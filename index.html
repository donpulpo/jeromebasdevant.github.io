Becoming AI-native is a hard choice,
not a tool purchase.

I help software and product companies rethink what they build — not just how they work — in a world where agents can do what your users used to do manually.

The real questions

Your team uses Cursor. Your engineers prompt Claude. You've seen the demos.

Which parts of your product do AI agents make obsolete? Which become more valuable? What should you build next — and what should you stop building entirely?

Those are the hard questions. Not adopting AI tools, but rethinking your product, your roadmap, and your value proposition in a world that's moving faster than your planning cycle.

Most companies skip this. They layer AI onto existing products and call it transformation. That works until it doesn't — and usually it shows up first in your retention numbers.

How I work

I work with leadership teams at software and product companies to make the shift from AI-curious to AI-native. Not with a strategy deck. With a structured process and hands-on work.

  • 01
    Diagnostic An honest look at where you actually stand — which parts of your product are at risk, which have leverage, and where your team's assumptions are wrong.
  • 02
    Strategy built on hard choices A clear point of view on what to build next. Not a list of opportunities — a prioritised bet, with the reasoning behind it.
  • 03
    Hands-on execution I work directly with your product and engineering teams. The change has to become real, not just credible on a slide.
